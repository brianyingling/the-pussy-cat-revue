What is the Pussycat Revue?

A long, long time ago...in a time before television, the internet, and cellphones...live entertainment was king. If you wanted to see a show you had to leave the house. Shows from this era were often a mishmash of song, dance, comedy, music, sketches, magic, circus, and more. This style of entertainment called vaudeville reigned supreme for over 50 years but quickly fell into disfavor after the advent of talking pictures and television. People say that “variety is still dead.” Meanwhile, millions are watching shows such as America’s Got Talent and Dancing with the Stars. New York City has had a steadily growing neoburlesque and variety movement since the late 1990’s. Currently there are numerous small shows running in these genres. However there is nothing on the grand scale of the shows from variety’s heyday.

The Pussycat Revue is a pastiche of genres which never co-existed under a single roof in the past. However what we are trying to do is throw a show the likes of which New York City hasn’t seen in over 80 years. We are going to present traditional variety acts, classic burlesque dance, and swing music in a Broadway-size spectacle. In addition, The Pussycat Revue will feature all live music. Much more than a show The Pussycat Revue is an attempt to bring people back to a classier time. When people dressed up to go out, and men took off their hats inside. The old- style nightclub atmosphere, and classic cocktails at The Pussycat Revue alone would make for a fine evening out.

  • Gypsy Rose Lee

    Gypsy Rose Lee is a name that many people know. She was the most famous stripper of all time, and a star of the Minsky Stage.

  • Billy Minsky's Burlesque

    Minsky was one of the most famous names in burlesque. The Republic on 42nd St. was their flagship location. For the first time burlesque was featured side-by-side with Broadway shows. Today Minsky’s Republic is the New Victory Theater which is New York City’s largest theater for children and families. A burlesque show from that era was on the scale of a Broadway show unlike the small cabaret shows of today. The goal of The Pussy Cat Revue is to bring back burlesque and variety to the glory days of yesteryear.

  • Ziegfeld Midnight Frolic

    The Ziegfeld Midnight Frolic is one of many productions that inspired The Pussycat Revue. It was a racy late-night show featuring some of the biggest talent of the time. It was intended for people to go to after a Broadway show. What is there for you to do today in Times Square when you get out of a show? New York City needs a show like The Pussycat Revue!

  • The Palace Theater

    The Palace was the most famous of all Vaudeville theaters.  It was built in 1913 by E.F. Albee and Martin Beck to be the flagship location of their chain. Playing at the Palace was considered a mark of success you truly made it in vaudeville once you played the Palace. The Palace still stands today, and currently a Broadway house. Interestingly it is one of only three current Broadway theaters whose street address is on Broadway.

  • Harry Houdini

    Houdini on the other hand is a name so famous that even children know it today. Many modern magicians are interested in recreating some of these classic stunts and doing their own interpretations.

  • Bill “Bojangles” Robinson

    Bojangles was the first African American to be a vaudeville star performing without blackface. He was famous for his stair dances. You may recognize him from the Shirley Temple films.
